HELIOS KPI GENERATION ENGINE

Purpose

The KPI Generation Engine is responsible for transforming structured datasets into business indicators (KPI).

This engine analyzes the dataset interpreted by HELIOS and automatically generates relevant indicators that help the user understand operational performance.

The objective is to convert raw data into meaningful analytical insights without requiring technical configuration from the user.

Core Responsibilities

The engine performs three main functions:

- Identify metrics within the dataset
- Generate possible KPI combinations
- Propose relevant business indicators

HELIOS assists this engine by understanding the context of the data and prioritizing the most meaningful indicators.

KPI Detection Process

After the dataset has been validated and interpreted, the engine identifies three fundamental data types:

Metrics
Numeric fields that can be aggregated.

Examples:
sales
cost
hours worked
quantity
revenue

Dimensions
Categorical fields used to group metrics.

Examples:
client
product
project
department
region

Temporal Dimensions
Date-related fields used for time analysis.

Examples:
date
month
year
period

These elements form the base structure used to generate indicators.

KPI Types

The engine generates several standard KPI types.

Total Indicators

These represent aggregated values.

Examples:
Total Sales
Total Costs
Total Hours Worked

Aggregation used:
SUM

Dimension-Based Indicators

Metrics grouped by dimensions.

Examples:
Sales by Client
Costs by Project
Hours by Department

Aggregation used:
SUM grouped by dimension

Time-Based Indicators

Metrics analyzed over time.

Examples:
Sales by Month
Revenue by Year
Hours by Week

This requires detection of temporal fields.

Average Indicators

Average values calculated from numeric fields.

Examples:
Average Sales
Average Cost per Project
Average Hours per Task

Aggregation used:
AVERAGE

Contribution Indicators

Percentage contribution relative to totals.

Examples:
Sales Contribution by Client
Cost Contribution by Department

Aggregation used:
percentage of total

Ranking Indicators

Indicators used to rank entities.

Examples:
Top Clients
Top Products
Projects with Highest Cost

Aggregation used:
sorting and ranking

KPI Prioritization

Not all generated indicators are equally useful.

HELIOS evaluates KPI relevance based on:

- dataset structure
- distribution of values
- business context patterns
- learned user behavior

This helps prioritize meaningful indicators and avoid excessive or irrelevant results.

KPI Library

All generated indicators are stored in a structured KPI library.

Example entries:

Total Sales
Total Costs
Sales by Client
Costs by Project
Hours by Department
Revenue by Month

The library allows dashboards and analyses to reuse previously generated indicators.

Custom KPI Creation

Users can define custom indicators when needed.

Examples:

Margin = Sales - Cost

Productivity = Hours Worked / Output

The system should support simple formulas without requiring programming knowledge.

KPI Engine Workflow

The KPI generation process follows this sequence:

Dataset validated
Dataset interpreted by HELIOS
Metrics identified
Dimensions identified
Temporal fields detected
Possible KPI combinations generated
Relevant KPI prioritized
KPI stored in KPI Library

These indicators are then passed to the Dashboard Generation Engine.

System Outcome

The KPI Generation Engine transforms structured data into operational indicators that help users understand their business performance.

This allows non-technical users to quickly obtain insights from their data without complex configuration.
